# Code of Conduct

## Short version

**Be kind. Be specific. Argue about ideas, not people. Assume good faith; extend it again after one bad interaction.**

This project exists because people are willing to publish what worked and what didn't on real production deployments. That's only sustainable if contributors feel safe being wrong in public. So:

- **Disagreement is welcome.** "This is wrong and here's why" is the point of a technical guide.
- **Hostility is not.** Attack arguments, not the person making them. No personal insults, no "you obviously haven't read X," no grandstanding.
- **Inclusivity is load-bearing.** Nothing in this guide should assume the reader is a particular gender, nationality, employer, age, or income bracket.
- **Privacy matters.** Don't paste someone else's logs, keys, or internal URLs without their explicit sign-off, even (especially) when demonstrating a problem.

## What to do if something goes wrong

1. **If you can, handle it directly and kindly.** Most conflicts are misreads; a quick "hey, that landed rough — did you mean X?" resolves 90%.
2. **If you can't, or if it's repeated, escalate to the maintainers.** Open a private vulnerability report (see [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md)) with `[Conduct]` in the title, or DM the maintainer listed on the project's X/Matrix profile.
3. **Expect a reply within 7 days.** We'll take the report seriously, investigate, and act. Responses range from a quiet conversation to permanent removal from the project — proportional to the conduct.

## Coverage

This applies in:

- GitHub issues, discussions, PRs, and reviews on this repo.
- Any community spaces that explicitly reference this guide (Discord threads, Matrix rooms, conference chats).
- Private DMs about this project.
